VibeMatch — Executive Summary
What It Does

VibeMatch takes a listener's taste preferences — favorite genre, mood, and energy level — and finds the best matching songs from a catalog. It shows that music recommendation isn't magic, it's math combined with thoughtful design choices.

How It Works

Each song is broken down into measurable traits: energy, emotional tone, and how acoustic vs. electronic it sounds. When you describe your taste, the system scores every song on how closely it matches your preferences, then returns the top 5. Exact genre or mood matches get bonus points. Songs scoring below 40/100 are filtered out, and results are diversified so you don't get 5 identical-sounding tracks.

What Makes It Interesting

It works great when preferences align naturally — ask for high-energy pop, get upbeat happy tracks. But it also exposes real AI limitations: it can recommend heavy metal to someone asking for "sad pop" because the numbers technically match even though the vibe is wrong. With only 18 songs, niche listeners get underserved.

The Bigger Lesson

VibeMatch reveals that even "smart" AI systems are following human-designed rules — and those rules reflect choices engineers made about what matters and how much.
